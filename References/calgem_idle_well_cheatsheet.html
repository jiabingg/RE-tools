🔄 Master Flow — Life of an Idle Well

📖 Key Definitions §1760 / PRC §3008

Idle Well (§1760(n)): Any well with 24 consecutive months of zero production (oil, gas, water for stimulation) or injection. Must produce/inject continuously for 6 months to exit idle status. Does not include active observation wells.
Long-Term Idle Well (§1760(o)): Any well idle for ≥ 8 years.
Low-Priority Idle Well (§1760(p)): Does NOT penetrate USDW, no surface pressure, not open to atmosphere, not in geologic hazard area, not critical/urban/environmentally sensitive.
USDW (§1760(x)): Underground Source of Drinking Water — aquifer not EPA-exempted that supplies or could supply a public water system, contains <10,000 mg/L TDS.
Freshwater (§1760(j)): Water with ≤ 3,000 mg/L TDS.
Urban Area (§1760(y)): Cohesive area of ≥ 25 establishments/residences; perimeter 300 ft beyond outer structures.
Partially Plugged (§1752): Well partially plugged with Division approval. Subject to casing pressure test every 60 months. Exempt from §1772.1 testing and §1772.1.2 engineering analysis.
Active Observation Well (PRC §3008(c)): Well used solely for gathering reservoir data (pressure, temperature). Must demonstrate need to Division and provide annual summary report.

📅 When Does a Well Become Idle?

Idle Start Date = first day of the month after the 24th consecutive month of zero reported volumes.

Example: Last production = Jan 2009 → Idle Start Date = Feb 1, 2011

A well exits idle when it is P&A'd per PRC §3208 or maintains 6 continuous months of production/injection. The exit date is the first day of the month after the 6th month.

⚠ Converting to an observation or monitoring well does NOT eliminate an idle well. Neither does transferring to another operator.

🏷️ Compliance vs. Roll-in Wells

Compliance Wells
Idle before April 1, 2019. Subject to Testing Compliance Work Plan §1772.1.4. Must complete all testing by April 1, 2025.
Roll-in Wells
Became idle after April 1, 2019. Testing deadlines triggered from date they become idle (24 mo. for fluid level & pressure test, 8 yr for clean out tag).

🧪 Three Required Tests §1772.1

1
Fluid Level Test Every 24 mo.
Acoustical, mechanical, or other reliable method to determine if fluid is above the base of USDW. If above USDW (or presumed to be), after 4/1/2025 must do casing pressure test within 90 days. Not required if wellbore does not penetrate USDW. If operator hasn't demonstrated USDW location, fluid is presumed above USDW.
2
Casing Pressure Test Within 24 mo.
Test from surface to 100 ft MD above uppermost perf, above deepest cemented casing shoe, or above top of landed liner — whichever is highest. Retest interval depends on test pressure (see Pressure Test tab). If the well cannot be safely tested, it is deemed failed.
3
Clean Out Tag Within 8 years
Run open-ended tubing or gauge ring of minimum P&A diameter to current Division-approved depth. Must reach at least 25 ft below uppermost perf in lowermost non-abandoned zone. Retest every 48 months (or less frequent with Division approval). Division may require more frequent tests if field/geologic conditions warrant.
📋 All testing requires 24-hour prior notice to district office. Test results submitted digitally within 60 days (30 days if fluid level above USDW).

🔓 Exemptions from Testing

  • Well on approved Testing Waiver Plan (scheduled for P&A within 8 years) — §1772.2
  • Well on approved IWMP scheduled for P&A — §1772.3
  • Partially plugged wells tested per §1752 (pressure test every 60 mo.) — §1752(e)
  • Wells penetrating gas storage reservoir tested under §1726.6 — §1772.7
  • Inaccessible wells with Division-approved demonstration & monitoring plan — §1772.1(d)
  • Wells where no part of wellbore is within ½ mile of USDW get 2 extra years before becoming "idle" for testing purposes — §1772.1(e)

👁️ Active Observation Wells §1772.5

Within 6 months of becoming an observation well (unless tested in past 5 years): casing pressure test per §1772.1.1. Retest every 60 months. Same remedies as idle wells if test fails.

Pre-existing (as of 4/1/2019): Test ≥ half by 4/1/2021, test all by 4/1/2023.

📝 Idle Well Inventory & Evaluation §1772

Due Jan 31 annually (initial due Jan 31, 2021). Must include for each idle well:

  • API number & name, spud date
  • Surface obstacles or access impediments
  • Most recent MI test results (type, date, results, remediation)
  • Whether well penetrates freshwater or USDW
  • Current tubing/casing pressures (or if open to atmosphere)
  • Critical well / urban area / environmentally sensitive wellhead status
  • Known geologic hazards in area
  • Known downhole issues (holes, collapsed casing, stuck equipment)
  • Whether partially plugged per §1752

🔧 Pressure Testing Parameters §1772.1.1

Standard Casing Pressure Test

  • Test with liquid unless Division approves gas
  • Wellbore filled with stable fluid column, free of excess gases
  • Minimum 200 psi above surface pressure
  • Calibrated gauge, accuracy within 1% of test pressure
  • Pressure recorded at least once per minute
  • Pass criteria: ≤ 3% pressure change over continuous 30 minutes
  • Steam exception: If in area of review for cyclic/steamflood, up to 10% increase is a pass
  • Analog gauge: test pressure must be within mid-range scale
  • Results submitted digitally within 60 days in tabular format

🔬 Alternative Test Methods

Inert Gas Depression Test (§1772.1.1(b)): Displace fluid to 100 ft above perf/shoe/liner top using inert gas. Minimum computed pressure must be ≥ 500 psi. Pass = fluid static, pressure stabilizes ±1% over 60 minutes. Confirm fluid level at end of test.
Alternate Mechanical Integrity Test (§1772.1.1(c)): E.g., magnetic flux or ultrasonic casing wall thickness inspection, ultrasonic imaging + cement evaluation log. Requires Division case-by-case approval as "at least as effective" as pressure test.
Passive Testing — Low-Priority Wells Only (§1772.1.1(d)): Caliper survey with Division-approved protocols for evaluating well integrity. Only for wells that meet ALL low-priority criteria.

⚠️ What Happens When a Test Fails? §1772.1(b)

Within 12 months of a failed casing pressure test or clean out tag (or any other non-compliance), the operator must do one of the following:

A
Bring the well into compliance
Fix the issue and pass the required test
B
Partially plug & abandon per §1752
Partial P&A still requires pressure testing every 60 months
C
Fully plug & abandon per PRC §3208
Permanently seal the well
D
Schedule for P&A under approved IWMP or Testing Waiver Plan
Commits operator to P&A within 8 years
⚠ Automatic Failure: If a well cannot be safely and effectively tested for any reason, it is deemed to have failed the pressure test. §1772.1(a)(2)
⚠ Each untested well = separate violation if operator misses Testing Compliance Work Plan benchmarks. §1772.1.4(f)

📜 Enforcement Consequences

Failure to pay fees (PRC §3206(c)): Conclusive evidence of well desertion → Supervisor may order P&A under §3237.
Failure to comply with regulations (PRC §3206.1(e)): Conclusive evidence of desertion → Supervisor may order P&A.
Failure to remediate or prove viability (PRC §3206.1(d)): Operator shall P&A per §3208.
Late fee penalties: 10% penalty + 1.5%/month non-compounding interest. May be referred to State Controller's Office after 2 months.
Since 1977, CalGEM has plugged and abandoned about 1,400 deserted wells at a cost of $29.5 million to the State.

📋 Testing Waiver Plan §1772.2

Purpose: Schedule P&A of idle wells within up to 8 years. While on an approved plan and in compliance, the operator is exempt from testing requirements (§1772.1, §1772.1.1, §1772.1.2).

Requirements

  • List of wells with API, name, scheduled P&A date, known integrity deficiencies
  • At least 10% of wells P&A'd each year
  • All wells P&A'd within 8 years
  • Prioritize per §1772.4 factors with written justification
  • Division may adjust order of wells
If operator fails to meet schedule: Division may cancel the entire plan. Operator must then test ALL listed wells within 90 days. No new waiver plan until fully in compliance with §1772.1.

📑 Idle Well Management Plan (IWMP) §1772.3 / PRC §3206

Purpose: Filed in lieu of paying idle well fees (due May 1 each year). Commits operator to eliminating idle wells annually. Up to 8-year plan, reviewed annually by CalGEM. IWMP filed after May 1 will NOT be accepted.

AB 1866 Changes (Effective Jan 1, 2025)

Key change: IWMPs now cover ALL idle wells, not just long-term idle wells. Minimum annual elimination percentages increased. Operators must consider prioritization factors when scheduling wells for P&A.

📊 Prioritization Factors §1772.4

When prioritizing wells for testing or P&A on any plan, operators shall consider:

  • Critical well, urban area, or environmentally sensitive wellhead
  • Known geologic hazards (subsidence, landslides, seismicity damage history)
  • Surface pressure in casing/tubing, or open to atmosphere
  • Surface obstacles preventing access
  • Known downhole issues (holes in casing, collapsed casing, stuck rods/packer/fish)
  • Fluid level above base of freshwater
  • Fluid level above base of USDW
  • Age of the idle well
  • Other threats to life, health, property, natural resources
  • Operational/economic efficiencies from sequencing

📐 15-Year Engineering Analysis §1772.1.2

Trigger: By the end of the month in which an idle well has been idle for 15 years. Must demonstrate to Division's satisfaction it is viable to return the well to operation.

Required Elements — Reservoir Viability (§1772.1.2(b))

  • API number and well name
  • Statement of potential future use
  • Identification of each reservoir unit & reservoir characteristics
  • Representative e-log below deepest producing zone — all geologic units, formations, USDWs, freshwater aquifers, oil/gas zones
  • Structural contour map on geologic marker near top of each reservoir unit — faults, lateral containment, productive zone extent

Required Elements — Mechanical Integrity (§1772.1.2(c) / §1772.1.3)

  • Complete casing diagram: operator, lease, API, spud date, ground elevation, reference elevation, USDW/freshwater bases
  • Casing sizes/grades/connections/weights, shoe/stub/liner depths
  • Perforations, open-hole completions, water shutoffs, cement ports, casing damage, junk/fish
  • Subsurface equipment (SSVs, packers, gas lift mandrels)
  • Hole diameters, cement plugs & fill locations (top & bottom)
  • Fluid type/weight between cement plugs
  • Formation/zone/marker depths, injection zone & confining layers
  • Cement yield calculations, slurry info (volume, density, yield, type, additives)
  • Wellbore path (MD + inclination & azimuth)
  • Current passing pressure test + clean out tag results

If Division Determines "Not Viable"

Process: Division provides basis for determination → operator gets ≥ 30 days to provide additional info → if still not viable after final review → operator must P&A within 12 months or schedule under approved plan.
Multi-well efficiency: If reservoir data applies to multiple wells in same field, operator can reference it in subsequent analyses without resubmitting. §1772.1.2(e)
For wells idle ≥ 9 years as of 4/1/2019: analysis due by LATER of (A) 60 days after scheduled testing in Compliance Work Plan, or (B) end of month when well hits 15 years idle. §1772.1.2(i)

💰 Annual Idle Well Fees PRC §3206 — Eff. 1/1/2025 (AB 1866)

AB 1866 (Sept 2024) dramatically increased idle well fees. Fees are assessed for each well idle at any point in the prior calendar year. The operator as of Dec 31 of the prior year is responsible.
