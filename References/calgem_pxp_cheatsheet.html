Underground Injection Control — Project-by-Project Review · Non-Expansion · Periodic Review

📋 What Is the UIC Program?

CalGEM's Underground Injection Control (UIC) Program administers state regulations for permitting, drilling, inspecting, testing, and sealing Class II injection wells in California. CalGEM has EPA-delegated primacy for Class II wells under the Safe Drinking Water Act. The program coordinates with the State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) via the Revised Memorandum of Agreement (MOA), last updated March 18, 2026.

Your Three Core Workflows: Project-by-Project Review (full data package for new/expansion/merge), Non-Expansion NOI review (adding wells to approved projects), and Periodic Review (every 3 years minimum per §1724.6(d)).

The flowchart below shows the review process for New, Merge, Expansion, and Project-by-Project reviews per Attachment 1 of the revised MOA appendix.

Key Participants: Operator/Consultant → CalGEM Technical Lead → CalGEM Project Manager → CalGEM HQ (UIC Program Review) → Water Board → CalGEM CEQA. The District Supervisor provides approval at step 4.3 and final approval at step 7.

📂 Three Review Pathways at a Glance

Project-by-Project Review
Full data package for new projects, expansions, or merges. Uses Attachment 2 checklist (March 2026). Requires complete Engineering Study, Geologic Study, and Injection Plan.
Non-Expansion AOR
Adding wells (NOIs) to already-approved projects. Uses Attachment 3 checklist (Dec 2021). Focused on new well AOR, wellbore integrity, and incremental impact assessment.
Periodic Review
Per §1724.6(d), CalGEM reviews existing projects at least every 3 years to verify compliance and ensure PAL conditions remain effective.

⚖️ Non-Expansion vs. Full Review: Key Differences

🔁 Periodic Review — Every 3 Years Minimum

Per §1724.6(d), CalGEM reviews existing UIC projects periodically, not less than once every three years, to verify compliance with regulations and PAL conditions, and to ensure conditions remain effective in preventing damage to life, health, property, and natural resources.

PALs are subject to suspension, modification, or rescission by the Division. If the Division determines operation is inconsistent with the subchapter or PAL conditions, or poses a threat, injection must cease immediately upon written notice §1724.6(e).

What CalGEM Checks During Periodic Review

  1. 1
    PAL Compliance: Are all PAL conditions being met? Are injection rates, pressures, and volumes within approved limits? Are all wells on the approved well list?
  2. 2
    MIT Status: Are all Part 1 (casing integrity) and Part 2 (fluid migration) tests current per the required schedules? Any wells that lost injection approval automatically? §1724.10(i)(4)
  3. 3
    MASIP Compliance: Are surface injection pressures within approved MASIP values? Continuous pressure monitoring records reviewed §1724.10.4.
  4. 4
    Data Currency: Is the supporting data (pressure, temperature, water quality, maps) current and accurately reflective of the project setting? §1724.7(a)
  5. 5
    AOR Evaluation: Has the AOR changed due to updated reservoir conditions, new wells, or pressure changes? Any new concerns about fluid migration?
  6. 6
    Well Integrity: Any wells in the AOR that have developed integrity concerns? Any new P&A wells that need evaluation? §1724.8
  7. 7
    Surface Expressions: Any surface expressions reported? SEMPP current (if applicable)? §1724.11
  8. 8
    Injection Reports: Monthly injection reports filed on time? §1724.10(c) Chemical analysis filed when source changed? §1724.10(d)

⏱️ Key Timelines & Deadlines for Periodic Compliance

🚨 Automatic Cease-Injection Triggers §1724.13

The operator must cease injection immediately and notify CalGEM if any of the following occur:

  1. !
    MIT not performed as required, or results not submitted
  2. !
    MIT failed or any indication of loss of mechanical integrity
  3. !
    Failure, breach, or hole in tubing/packer/cement/casing (including above packer)
  4. !
    Visible surface damage or erosion caused by injection
  5. !
    Any indication fluids not confined to approved injection zone
  6. !
    Indication of damage to life, health, property, natural resources, or loss of hydrocarbons
  7. !
    PRC §3227 information not provided
  8. !
    Well becomes idle (PRC §3008(d)), unless Division grants allowance
  9. !
    Division instructs suspension in writing

🔧 Mechanical Integrity Testing (MIT)

Two-part demonstration required for all injection wells §1724.10(i). 48-hour advance notice to District required. Digital results within 60 days.

Part 1 — Casing Integrity (SAPT) §1724.10.1

Casing pressure test at MASIP. Required before first injection, after equipment repositioning, and on schedule:

Waterflood
5 yr
Water Disposal
5 yr
Steamflood
5 yr
Cyclic Steam
5 yr
Gas Injection
5 yr
Gas Disposal
1 yr
Pass criteria: ≤3% pressure change over continuous 30 minutes. Cyclic steam: up to 10% increase is acceptable. Test at ≥200 psi above surface pressure. Calibrated gauge within 1% accuracy. Record at least 1/min.

Part 2 — Fluid Migration §1724.10.2

Required within 3 months after first injection, then on schedule:

Waterflood
2 yr
Water Disposal
1 yr
Steamflood
2 yr
SF w/ Tubing & Packer
5 yr
Cyclic Steam
2 yr
Low-Use Cyclic
5 yr
Methods: R/A Tracer Survey (while injecting), Temperature Survey (off injection 24–48 hrs), Noise Log (200 ft repeat section), or Division-approved alternative. Also triggered by >25% unplanned pressure variance in 48 hours.

📈 MASIP Calculation §1724.10.3

MASIP is the LOWER of: (1) the calculated value above, or (2) the initial test pressure from the most recent successful MIT Part 1 casing test. If friction loss is factored in, use new coated tubing of largest diameter. Division may approve >0.95 multiplier on well-specific basis.

👥 RASCI Role Definitions — Attachment 4 (March 2026)

📖 Key Definitions §1720.1

TermDefinition
AORArea of Review — calculated lateral distance of pressure/temperature influence OR fixed ¼-mile radius. Proposed by operator, may be specified by Division.
FreshwaterWater containing ≤3,000 mg/L TDS.
USDWUnderground Source of Drinking Water — aquifer not EPA-exempted that supplies (or could supply) a public water system and contains <10,000 mg/L TDS.
Injection ZoneDefined 3D space with fixed boundaries where injected fluid is anticipated to occupy.
UIC ProjectSustained/recurring injection into one or more wells over extended period into approved zone (EOR, disposal, storage, pressure maintenance, subsidence mitigation).
PALProject Approval Letter — written record of Division's approval including conditions.
MASIPMaximum Allowable Surface Injection Pressure — per §1724.10.3 calculation.
ZEIZone of Endangering Influence — per 40 CFR §146.6.
Surface ExpressionFlow/release from subsurface to surface outside a wellbore that appears caused by injection operations.
Low-Use CyclicCyclic steam well with ≤24 days injection/yr AND ≤12,000 bbl/yr (past 5 yrs) AND no surface expressions on project.
Mechanical IntegrityAll mechanical well barriers reliably perform containing pressure and are free from leakage.
SEMPPSurface Expression Monitoring and Prevention Plan — required for projects with known surface expressions or steam into diatomite.
