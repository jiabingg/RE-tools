CalGEM Well Activity Permits

Regulatory Requirements Guide — New Drill, Sidetrack, Rework, Deepen, Abandon, Re-Abandon
PRC §3203–§3205 CCR §1720–§1724 CCR §1722–§1723 OG106 / OG150 / OG160

New Drill — Drill a New Well Hole

Definition
Drilling a new wellbore from the surface into the earth for the purpose of producing oil, gas, or geothermal resources, or for injection, observation, or disposal. This is the most comprehensively reviewed permit type under CalGEM.

📋 Regulatory Requirements PRC §3203

  • Operator must file a written Notice of Intention (NOI) before commencing drilling — no work until CalGEM approves PRC §3203(a)
  • CalGEM has 10 working days from receipt to respond; silence = deemed approved PRC §3203(a)
  • NOI valid for 24 months from date CalGEM received it — cannot be extended; deemed cancelled if not commenced PRC §3203(a)
  • Must designate a California-resident agent before filing NOI OG150
  • CEQA compliance must be completed prior to submitting NOI PRC §3203.5
  • Must file an individual indemnity bond with the NOI, or have a blanket bond on file PRC §3204
  • All operations must comply with CalGEM Field Rules for the applicable field CCR §1722(k)
  • Protection of all hydrocarbon zones and freshwater required via proper casing and cementing CCR §1722.2–§1722.4
  • Adequate blowout prevention equipment (BOPE) required per MO7 CCR §1722.5
  • Well spacing must conform to applicable spacing rules CCR §1721–§1721.9
  • Spill contingency plan must be on file CCR §1722(b)
  • Copy of local land use authorization required with NOI submission PRC §3203.5

🔄 Permit Process Flow

1

Designate CA Agent

File OG150 + OGD7 with district office. Agent must be an individual residing in CA.

2

Complete CEQA

Obtain local land-use permits. Secure Notice of Determination or Exemption before filing NOI.

3

Submit NOI (OG106) + Bond (OG160)

File in duplicate with district office. Attach drilling program, wellbore schematic, lease map, CEQA document, bond.

4

CalGEM Review (10 working days)

CalGEM examines completeness, field rule compliance, freshwater protection, BOPE adequacy, well spacing, CEQA. Issues P-Report if approved.

5

Post P-Report & Commence Operations

P-Report must be posted at drill site at all times. Comply with all mandatory tests/inspections listed.

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Completeness: All required forms, attachments, and data present (drilling program, wellbore schematic, lease map, CEQA doc, bond)
  • Field Rule Compliance: Proposed casing, depth, spacing, and completion consistent with established field rules; deviations must be justified
  • Freshwater Protection: Adequate casing program and cementing plan to protect all freshwater aquifers and hydrocarbon zones
  • Well Spacing: Proposed location meets well spacing patterns under CCR §1721–§1721.9 for the applicable pool
  • BOPE Adequacy: Blowout prevention equipment plan meets MO7 requirements; critical/high-pressure wells may require BOP control plan
  • CEQA Compliance: Valid local land-use authorization and environmental review on file; local permit expiration dates checked
  • Bonding: Individual bond amount correct for proposed depth, or valid blanket bond on file
  • Operator Standing: No outstanding violations, unpaid civil penalties, or unresolved compliance orders that would trigger denial under PRC §3203(c)
  • Drilling Fluid Program: Appropriate weight, type, and properties for anticipated downhole conditions
  • Directional Survey Plan: Adequate survey plan to confirm well path within permitted boundaries

New Drill — Knowledge Quiz

Sidetrack — Cement & Extend New Path

Definition
A sidetrack involves cementing a portion of the original wellbore and drilling a new path (lateral) from the uncemented portion of the original well. Under CalGEM regulations, a sidetrack is classified as a rework/redrill operation because it permanently alters the well's casing and function. CCR §1720(b)

📋 Regulatory Requirements CCR §1720(b), PRC §3203(b)

  • Classified as a rework/redrill — requires a new NOI (OG106) submitted after initial well completion PRC §3203(b)
  • Must include detailed wellbore schematic showing the cement plug placement, kickoff point, and new wellbore trajectory
  • CEQA document required if the sidetrack changes the well's field, area, or completion zone
  • All casing, cementing, and freshwater protection requirements apply to the new wellbore section CCR §1722.2–§1722.4
  • Adequate BOPE required; ALL operations in urban areas require BOPE CCR §1722.5, MO7
  • P-Report must be posted at well site during operations CCR §1722(e)
  • Operations must not deviate from approved program without prior Division approval, except in emergency CCR §1722(g)
  • Operator must maintain well control at all times, with or without permit PRC §3219
  • Bond on file must be adequate for the new well depth after deepening through sidetrack
  • If sidetrack results in a new field/area, the change must be noted on the NOI

📝 Forms & Information Required

FormPurposeKey Information Needed
OG106 Notice of Intention (Rework/Redrill) Original well history, proposed kickoff depth, cement plug interval, new hole trajectory (inclination/azimuth), target zone, updated wellbore schematic, casing/cementing program for new section, BOPE plan
OG160 Bond (if depth increases) Updated bond if new total depth crosses the 10,000 ft threshold ($25k → $40k)
CEQA Doc If required Required when sidetrack changes completion zone, field, or area
Directional Survey Post-drill requirement Must demonstrate new wellbore path is within permitted boundaries and spacing requirements

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Cement Plug Integrity: Proposed cement plug placement sufficient to isolate the abandoned portion of the original wellbore
  • New Trajectory Plan: Kickoff point, build rate, target azimuth, and landing zone clearly defined and achievable
  • Casing & Cementing: New hole section has adequate casing and cement to protect hydrocarbon zones and freshwater
  • Well Spacing: The new bottom-hole location complies with well spacing rules for the target pool
  • BOPE Plan: Adequate for the new hole section, especially if drilling through different pressure regimes
  • Existing Wellbore Condition: Evidence that the existing casing above the kickoff point is mechanically sound
  • Field Rule Compliance: New completion zone consistent with field rules; deviations must be justified
  • Bond Adequacy: Bond amount correct for the new measured/true vertical depth
💡 Note: A sidetrack that results in a significant change in well trajectory may effectively create a "new well" from a regulatory perspective. CalGEM may require additional data comparable to a new drill permit if the departure from the original wellbore is substantial.

Sidetrack — Knowledge Quiz

Rework — Re-enter for Secondary Activities

Definition — CCR §1720(b)
"Rework" means any operation subsequent to drilling that involves deepening, redrilling, plugging, or permanently altering in any manner the casing of a well or its function. This includes recompletions, zone changes, stimulation requiring casing modification, squeeze cementing, casing repair, and perforation changes.

📋 Regulatory Requirements PRC §3203(b)

  • Requires a new NOI (OG106) filed in duplicate before commencing any rework PRC §3203(b)
  • NOI valid for 24 months from receipt; operations once commenced must be completed in a timely and orderly manner CCR §1722(d)
  • Wellbore schematic diagram must be included showing current well state and proposed changes
  • CEQA document required if the rework changes the well's completion zone, field, or area
  • BOPE required; ALL operations in urban areas require BOPE regardless of well type CCR §1722.5
  • P-Report must be posted at well site during all operations CCR §1722(e)
  • Give district office sufficient advance notice for required inspections and tests CCR §1722(f)
  • Operations shall not deviate from approved program without prior Division approval except in emergency CCR §1722(g)
  • Operator must maintain well control during ANY operation, with or without permit PRC §3219
  • Blowouts, fires, serious accidents, significant gas/water leaks must be promptly reported to district office CCR §1722(i)
  • Changes in well number or designation require written supervisor consent PRC §3203(b)

📝 Forms & Information Required

FormPurposeKey Information Needed
OG106 Notice of Intention (Rework) Current well configuration, proposed rework operations (recompletion zone, new perforations, squeeze cement, casing repair, etc.), updated wellbore schematic, BOPE plan, anticipated timeline
CEQA Doc If required Needed when changing completion zone, field, or area
Wellbore Schematic Mandatory attachment Current casing intervals/sizes, perforation locations, cement status, proposed changes highlighted

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Scope of Work: What exactly is being changed — perforations, casing, completion zone, function?
  • Wellbore Integrity: Current casing and cement condition adequate to support the proposed rework
  • Zone Isolation: Will the rework maintain proper isolation between productive zones and freshwater?
  • BOPE Adequacy: Equipment rated for the well's pressure regime, especially for wells with H₂S or high pressure
  • Field Rule Compliance: Proposed new completion zone consistent with field rules
  • Well Control Plan: Especially for critical or high-pressure wells requiring a BOP and control plan
  • Environmental Compliance: CEQA and spill contingency plan current
  • Operator Standing: No outstanding violations or unpaid penalties
✅ Key Distinction: Routine maintenance (e.g., rod/pump changes, wireline logging) that does NOT permanently alter the casing or well function does NOT require an NOI. However, any operation that deepens, redrills, plugs, or permanently alters the casing or its function requires an OG106.

Rework — Knowledge Quiz

Deepen — Extend Well Depth

Definition
Deepening is the operation of drilling below the current total depth of an existing well to access a deeper formation or zone. Under CalGEM, deepening is explicitly listed as a type of rework operation under CCR §1720(b) and requires an NOI per PRC §3203(b).

📋 Regulatory Requirements PRC §3203(b), CCR §1720(b)

  • Requires a new NOI (OG106) before commencing — deepening is explicitly covered by PRC §3203(b) PRC §3203(b)
  • Full drilling program for the deeper section required, including anticipated formations, pressures, and hazards
  • Casing and cementing program must protect all newly penetrated hydrocarbon zones and freshwater CCR §1722.2–§1722.4
  • Drilling fluid program must be adequate for deeper pressure regime CCR §1722.6
  • BOPE must be adequate for the deeper anticipated pressures CCR §1722.5
  • If new total depth crosses 10,000 ft, an updated bond is required ($25k → $40k individual) PRC §3204
  • Directional survey required for the deepened section CCR §1722.7
  • Well spacing compliance for the new target zone CCR §1721
  • CEQA required if targeting a new field or area
  • P-Report posted at well site during all operations CCR §1722(e)
  • Operations conducted in accordance with good oilfield practice CCR §1722(a)

📝 Forms & Information Required

FormPurposeKey Information Needed
OG106 Notice of Intention (Deepen) Current total depth, proposed new total depth, target zone/formation, drilling program for deeper section, updated casing/cementing plan, updated wellbore schematic, BOPE plan, drilling fluid program, directional survey plan
OG160 Updated Bond (if needed) New bond required if depth crosses 10,000 ft threshold. Bond amount: $40,000 individual for wells ≥10,000 ft
CEQA Doc If required Needed when deepening targets a new field/area or changes the well's environmental footprint
Wellbore Schematic Updated diagram Showing existing casing, proposed deeper hole section, new casing strings, cementing plan, target perforations

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Target Formation: What zone is being targeted? Is it within an established field, or does it represent a wildcat target?
  • Existing Wellbore Condition: Is the current casing mechanically sound enough to support deepening operations?
  • Casing & Cementing Plan: Adequate to protect all zones penetrated between old TD and new TD, including freshwater
  • Pressure Management: Drilling fluid weight and BOPE adequate for deeper pressure regimes
  • Well Spacing: Bottom-hole location at new depth complies with spacing rules for the target pool
  • Bond Adequacy: Must be updated if the new depth crosses the 10,000 ft threshold
  • Directional Survey Plan: Adequate to confirm the wellbore stays within permitted boundaries at the new depth
  • Field Rules: Proposed deeper completion consistent with field rules for that formation
  • Hazard Assessment: Anticipated H₂S, abnormal pressures, lost circulation zones in the deeper section
⚠️ Bond Alert If deepening takes the well past 10,000 ft measured depth, the individual bond amount increases from $25,000 to $40,000 (PRC §3204). The updated bond must be filed with the NOI. Failure to file an adequate bond can result in permit denial.

Abandon — Permanently Plug & Seal

Definition
Plugging and abandonment (P&A) is the permanent plugging and sealing of a well so that it will not produce fluids, endanger freshwater, or create a surface hazard. It involves placing cement plugs across specific intervals, mudding the hole, and cutting casing below the surface. CCR §1723–§1723.9

📋 Regulatory Requirements CCR §1723–§1723.8

  • Requires a new NOI (OG106) before commencing any plugging operations PRC §3203(b)
  • Oil/Gas Zone Plugs (§1723.1): Cement plug from 100 ft below to 100 ft above each oil/gas zone (open hole), or 100 ft above top perforations/liner/cementing point (cased hole)
  • Freshwater Protection (§1723.2): 200-ft cement plug across all fresh-saltwater interfaces (open hole); 100-ft plug inside casing across interface (cased hole); squeeze cement if needed
  • Casing Shoe Plug (§1723.3): Cement plug from 50 ft below to 50 ft above any casing shoe where hole is open below
  • Surface Plug (§1723.5): 25-ft minimum cement plug at surface in all annuli; casing cut off 5–10 ft below surface; steel plate welded on top showing API number
  • Hole Fluid (§1723(b)): Mud of proper weight placed across all intervals not plugged with cement; surface poured into all open annuli
  • BOPE may be required during P&A; requirements set by district deputy on P-Report CCR §1723(e)
  • Diligent effort required to recover junk before plugging; 100-ft plug on top of irrecoverable junk CCR §1723(f)
  • Division must witness and approve key plugging operations CCR §1723.7
  • Environmental inspection required after completion of plugging CCR §1723.7(h)

📝 Forms & Information Required

FormPurposeKey Information Needed
OG106 Notice of Intention (P&A) Detailed wellbore schematic showing: all casing intervals/sizes, perforation locations, proposed cement plug depths inside casing, cement locations outside casing, fresh-saltwater interfaces, proposed plug placement sequence
Wellbore Schematic Critical attachment Must show complete current well configuration including all casing strings, perforations, liners, cement behind casing, proposed plug depths, and surface plug detail

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Oil/Gas Zone Isolation: Proposed plugs cover all producing/penetrated oil and gas zones per §1723.1
  • Freshwater Protection: Plugs across all fresh-saltwater interfaces; squeeze cementing where cement behind casing is absent or inadequate
  • Casing Shoe Plugs: All casing shoes with open hole below are properly plugged
  • Surface Plug Plan: Adequate surface plugging with proper casing cutoff depth and steel plate identification
  • Hole Fluid Plan: Proper weight mud in all unplugged intervals
  • Junk in Hole: Any junk identified and plan to recover or plug around it
  • Radioactive Materials: If lost radioactive sources, special plugging per §1723(g) with red-dyed cement and whipstock
  • Division Witnessing Schedule: Key operations (zone plugs, freshwater protection, surface plug) require Division witnessing
  • Plugging Method: Bailer not permitted below 3,000 ft; surface pours only in holes ≥5 in. diameter (§1723(c)–(d))
  • Environmental Compliance: Post-P&A environmental inspection to ensure site remediation per Subchapter 2

Abandon — Knowledge Quiz

Re-Abandon — Re-Plug & Re-Seal

Definition
Re-abandonment is the operation of re-entering a previously plugged and abandoned well to replace, supplement, or correct cement plugs and re-seal the well. This is typically required when an existing P&A is found to be leaking, inadequate, or does not meet current regulatory standards. Re-abandonment follows the same NOI and P&A requirements as an original abandonment. PRC §3203(b), CCR §1723

📋 Regulatory Requirements

  • Requires a new NOI (OG106) — same form used for re-entering to replace cement plugs and re-abandon PRC §3203(b)
  • All original P&A requirements under §1723–§1723.8 apply to the re-abandonment
  • Wellbore schematic must show: existing plugs (as found), proposed new/replacement plugs, any removal or drill-out of old plugs
  • BOPE may be required for re-entry operations; determined by district deputy
  • If old plugs are found inadequate, CalGEM may require additional plugging beyond original requirements
  • Division witnessing required for key plugging operations per §1723.7
  • Environmental inspection required after completion
  • Surface completion must meet current standards: casing cut 5–10 ft below grade, steel plate with API number welded on
  • Re-abandonment may be ordered by CalGEM if a previously abandoned well is found to be leaking or posing a hazard
  • Wells in the AoR of injection projects may require re-abandonment if they lack 100/150 ft of verified cement above the injection zone CCR §1724.8

📝 Forms & Information Required

FormPurposeKey Information Needed
OG106 Notice of Intention (Re-Abandon) Original P&A history, reason for re-abandonment, current well condition (if known), proposed re-entry method, updated wellbore schematic showing existing and proposed plugs, drill-out plan for old plugs (if needed), new plugging sequence
Original P&A Records Reference documentation Original abandonment report, plug depths, cement volumes, any known deficiencies
Wellbore Schematic Updated diagram Must clearly distinguish between existing plugs and proposed new/replacement plugs

🔍 What CalGEM Examines During Review

  • Reason for Re-Abandonment: Is this operator-initiated or CalGEM-ordered? What deficiency is being corrected?
  • Original P&A Assessment: What plugs are in place, and which are deficient or missing?
  • Re-Entry Plan: How will the old plugs be drilled out or bypassed? Is the approach safe given the well's age and condition?
  • Updated Plugging Plan: New plug placement must meet current standards under §1723–§1723.8
  • Freshwater Protection: Particular focus on freshwater interface plugging if original P&A predates current regulations
  • UIC AoR Compliance: If re-abandonment is triggered by injection project review, verified cement above injection zone
  • BOPE Requirements: Based on well's pressure history and current condition
  • Surface Completion: Must bring surface conditions up to current standards
⚠️ Important CalGEM may ORDER a re-abandonment at the operator's expense if a previously abandoned well is found to be leaking, contaminating groundwater, or creating a surface hazard. Wells abandoned under older, less stringent standards are particularly subject to re-abandonment orders when they fall within the Area of Review (AoR) of active injection projects.
