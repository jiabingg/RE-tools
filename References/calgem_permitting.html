🔄 Permitting Lifecycle — Master Flow

🛢️ Drilling New Wells — Step by Step

1
Designate a California Agent
Must be an individual (not a company) who resides in California. Serves as company contact for all correspondence, permits, and legal process. File form OG150 + OGD7 (Contact Questionnaire) with the district office where the well is located. Separate forms needed for each designated area.
2
Submit Notice of Intention (OG106)
Filed in duplicate with the appropriate CalGEM district office. Must include: complete drilling program, lease map/plat, lease description, proposed wellbore schematic, and a technical contact name + email. Must be consistent with CalGEM Field Rules — any deviation requires explanation and justification.
3
CEQA Compliance
Must be completed PRIOR to submitting the NOI. Usually involves permits from a local land-use agency. Attach a copy of the local jurisdiction's Notice of Determination or Notice of Exemption to the OG106.
4
Submit Bond (OG160)
Individual indemnity or cash bond must accompany each NOI unless a blanket bond is already on file. Individual bond amounts are based on well depth. Form OG160 replaces all prior bond forms.
5
Receive CalGEM Approval (P-Report)
CalGEM issues a Permit to Conduct Well Operations. The P-Report must be posted at the drill site at all times. It lists all mandatory tests and inspections that CalGEM inspectors must witness and approve.
⚠ 24-Month Expiration: If operations have not commenced within 24 months of CalGEM receiving the NOI, it is deemed canceled and cannot be extended. A Well Status Inquiry letter may be used to request a one-year extension prior to expiration. (PRC §3203(a))
📋 All new wells require: protection of all hydrocarbon zones and fresh waters through adequate casing & cementing, adequate blowout prevention equipment (MO7), and proper well spacing (§1721–1721.9 CCR).

🔧 Rework / Redrill Operations §1720(b)

Definition: "Rework" means any operation subsequent to drilling that involves deepening, redrilling, plugging, or permanently altering in any manner the casing of a well or its function.

Requirements

  • Submit OG106 (Notice of Intention) to the district office — filed in duplicate
  • Include wellbore schematic diagram and CEQA document (if required by local agency)
  • Note any changes in completion zone, Field, and/or Area for recompletions, redrills, or deepenings
  • Receive Permit to Rework/Redrill (P-Report) before commencing — must be posted at well site
  • Adequate BOPE required (see MO7). All operations in urban areas require BOPE regardless.
PRC §3219: Operators must maintain well control during ANY well operation, whether or not a permit is required.

🔒 Plugging & Abandonment

Before P&A Operations

  • Submit OG106 (replaces former OG108 and OG123)
  • Include wellbore schematic: casing intervals/sizes, perforation locations, cement plug depths inside casing, cement location outside casing
  • Also used for re-entering a well to replace cement plugs and re-abandon

Special Requirements

Radioactive Sources (§1722(j)): Loss of radioactive materials in a well (except tracers for injection surveys) must be promptly reported to the Department of Health Services at (916) 327-5106 and OES at 1-800-852-7550, plus the CalGEM district office. Special plugging instructions per §1723(g) will be issued.
⚠ Conductor Pipe: Once set, if not to be used (e.g., permit cancelled), it must be properly secured or covered to prevent falls. CalGEM must be contacted for plugging requirements to minimize contamination and surface hazards.

💧 Injection Well / UIC Project Approval §1724.6–§1724.13

Authority: CalGEM administers the UIC program for Class II wells under delegated primacy from the U.S. EPA through the Safe Drinking Water Act. Affects approximately 55,000 UIC wells in California.

Two Main Types of Injection Wells

Enhanced Recovery: Water or steam injection for EOR — waterflood, steamflood, cyclic steam, CO₂ EOR
Disposal: Return of produced briny water back into the underground source from which it came

Application Requirements (§1724.7)

  • Complete project application with data per CCR §1724.7 (onshore) or §1748.2 (offshore)
  • Engineering Study: AoR determination, map of all wells within/adjacent to AoR, casing diagrams, flood-pattern map
  • Geologic Study: Reservoir characteristics, structural contour maps, isopach maps, cross sections, representative e-log
  • Injection Plan: Purpose, facilities map, anticipated rates/volumes, monitoring system, injection method, chemical analysis of injection liquid
  • Individual well NOIs for all wells must be submitted and permits received before injection begins
  • Plans must be signed by owner, agent, or officer of the company
  • CalGEM consults with State/Regional Water Quality Control Board during review

Key Operational Requirements

  • Project Approval Letter (PAL): Required before any injection. Specifies approved injection zone, conditions. Reviewed at least every 3 years.
  • MIT Part 1 — Casing Integrity (§1724.10.1): Pressure test every 5 years (every 1 year for gas disposal wells). Min 200 psi above surface pressure. Pass = ≤3% change over 30 min.
  • MIT Part 2 — Fluid Migration (§1724.10.2): Radioactive tracer survey, temperature survey, or noise log every 2 years. Disposal wells = every 1 year. Low-use cyclic steam = every 5 years.
  • Continuous Pressure Monitoring (§1724.10.4): Well-specific injection pressure recorded at all times. Monthly reporting of highest instantaneous pressure.
  • MASIP: Surface injection pressure shall not exceed the maximum allowable, determined by step-rate test and fracture gradient calculation.
  • Injection reports filed monthly on or before the last day of each month for the preceding month.
⚠ Automatic Loss of Injection Approval: Any injection well not tested as required under §1724.10.1 and §1724.10.2 automatically loses approval. Written Division approval required to reinject. Each day of unauthorized injection = separate violation.

✅ Compliance & Regulations

Key Regulatory Packages (all effective as shown)

  • Idle Well Testing & Management (2019) — Fluid level tests, casing pressure tests, clean out tags, 15-year engineering analysis, testing compliance work plans
  • Underground Injection Control (2019) — Project approval letters, MIT Parts 1 & 2, continuous pressure monitoring, surface expression requirements, MASIP
  • Underground Gas Storage (2018) — Separate from UIC, covers gas storage wells under §1726–1726.10
  • Oil and Gas Pipelines (2018) — Pipeline integrity, leak detection, secondary containment

Operator Ongoing Obligations

  • Well Disposition/Transfer (OG30A): Notify CalGEM no later than the date of sale/transfer becoming final — both seller and buyer must notify
  • Confidential Status: Written request with justification, submitted to district office. Per PRC §3234 and CCR §1997.1
  • Well Maintenance Reporting: Operators must report well maintenance activities through WellSTAR
  • Production/Injection Reporting: Monthly reporting via WellSTAR per PRC §3227
  • Bonding: Must maintain valid bond (individual per well or blanket). Bond amounts based on well depth.

MOUs/MOAs

CalGEM + State Water Board (2018 revised): Coordinates UIC program for Class II wells, produced water discharges to land, enforcement, and incident response. Single-permit approach satisfying both agencies' requirements.

💰 Oil & Gas Assessments

What it is: A small statewide assessment on oil and gas produced in California. Supports CalGEM and certain other state entities. Levied per PRC Article 7, Division 3. There is NO statewide severance tax on oil and gas in California.

How It Works

  • Rate set each June based on CalGEM's estimated budget ÷ prior year total assessable production
  • Imposed on each barrel of oil and each 10,000 cubic feet of natural gas produced
  • Producers report monthly to CalGEM; file annual reconciliation report
  • Assessment Notices sent to each producer before June 15 each year
  • Assessment Roll delivered to State Controller on first Monday in July

Payment Schedule

Under $500: Full amount due on or before August 15
$500 or more: Half due by August 15, balance due by February 1
⚠ Substantial penalties and interest are levied on delinquent payments.
